My modern calligraphy - a *tea* story

Art is everywhere, everything is art. With that in mind, a different universe made of stories and feelings started to build up inside of me since I was a kid. One day, one of my friends who truly believed in me, gave me a little, but amazing ink quill as a gift. I remember that the gift excited me a lot, but all I did, when I got back home, was to leave it high and dry in a desk drawer. But little did I know that was only the beginning of what is now my lifelong creative journey...

On a hot summer day, a whole year later, I unpacked my ink quill. It took me hours and hours to understand how to use it... And even after I thought I can wield it, I ended up a lot of times with ink stains on my clothes and a bin loaded with crumpled paper full of bad lettering. One time, my father walked in on me while I was doing calligraphy sitting on my bed and warned me about the open ink bottle... but of course I spilled it all over my bed... It took a day and a half to dry out the stain of colored ink... lucky me: the bed was black!

some of my work that I once called "mistakes"

The days passed and I started to set up an alphabet to learn. Reproducing drawings and fonts seemed so simple when I looked at the images found on Tumblr or Pinterest... But to come up with something decent... that was another story.
My secret was to feel. To let the art that I've been bulding up inside of me truly come to life using my own hands. My emotions became my true inspiration. Even though I would reproduce some pieces, I started to do it in my own style. I started to come up with different creations (drawings or handwritten quotes) combining various techniques encountered.

I began to turn my feelings into art. That was my escape from a dull world. I began to appreciate the things around me and simplicity seemed to be the easiest form of art that could be reached. From every sunrise I caught, the colors of the book covers I read, to every cup of coffee my mother made for me in the morning, to my friends laughing on a warm evening, I found art in every little thing that surrounded me.
Creating in turn made me happy. And it still makes me happy even today.

Over the years, I ended up buying a lot of pens, pencils, colored ink and paper, pen heels and I became the "artsy" one of the group that always created birthday cards for her friends.

...and I handwrote on my walls some of my favorite quotes (and even rewrote it when my mom painted the wall in yellow)

And now I'm here. I don't know if my art is good or bad and I don't even want to know the answer. All I know is that art makes me smile, makes me wander on and on and it is, these days, a touching way of "showering" my boyfriend with love, by giving him a ton of love letters.
But I do still hope my art makes the ones that come across it feel their heart vibrate just like mine does when writing and drawing.

And that's the story of a girl who, besides anyone would say, expresses her feeling through art with her hands.
And that's the tea cup I've used to take the photos and the ending of my *tea* story.
